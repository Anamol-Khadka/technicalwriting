Real‑World Examples

Cafés, shops, and online businesses using data instead of guesswork.

Quick visual: as predictions improve, waste shrinks and useful insight grows over time.

Dunkin’ on Campus: Reducing Donut & Coffee Waste

At a campus Dunkin’, staff noticed that a lot of donuts and coffee were thrown away at the end of each day. They weren’t sure how demand was being predicted, so they decided to track it themselves.

  • They recorded donuts and coffee sold each hour.
  • They noted day of week and special events (game days, exams).
  • They measured how many items were left over at closing time.

Cause–effect: After two weeks, they saw that early afternoons were much slower than mornings.

  • They reduced donut production in the slow hours.
  • They brewed smaller coffee batches later in the day.

As a result, waste dropped while sales stayed stable. The prediction was simple: “mornings are strong, afternoons are weaker,” but putting numbers behind it made the change feel safe and reasonable.

Local Café: Planning for Fridays vs. Tuesdays

A neighborhood café assumed that “every day is busy.” When they checked their data, they discovered big differences.

  • Fridays averaged 150 pastry sales.
  • Tuesdays averaged only about 80 pastry sales.

Using this pattern, they used predictive analytics in a simple way: instead of baking the same 120 pastries every day, they prepared closer to 150 on Fridays and 80–90 on Tuesdays.

Result: Less pastry waste on Tuesdays and fewer sold‑out situations on Fridays.

Online Shop: Predicting Holiday Demand

A small online store selling custom phone cases wanted to prepare for the holiday rush without over‑ordering.

  • They pulled last year’s daily order data for November and December.
  • They spotted spikes during Black Friday week and the first two weeks of December.
  • They noticed a sharp drop after December 20th.

With these patterns, they ordered extra stock for the peak weeks and reduced orders for the slower late‑December days.

Analogy: It’s like learning which bus is always crowded and planning your travel time around it.
