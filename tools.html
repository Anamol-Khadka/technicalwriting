Tools You Can Use

Beginner‑friendly tools for doing predictive analytics without advanced coding.

Quick visual: as predictions improve, waste shrinks and useful insight grows over time.

Start Simple: Spreadsheets

You do not need expensive software to begin with predictive analytics. Spreadsheets are powerful and familiar to many people.

Excel & Google Sheets

  • Classification: General‑purpose tools for organizing numbers and building simple models.
  • What you can do:
    • Store and clean your daily sales data.
    • Calculate averages, totals, and growth rates.
    • Create line charts and bar charts for visual patterns.
    • Use formulas to forecast future demand.
Visual idea: A screenshot‑style mockup of a spreadsheet with columns “Date,” “Items Sold,” and “Forecast.”

Point‑of‑Sale (POS) Exports

Many point‑of‑sale systems — including small business‑friendly options — can export data as CSV or Excel files.

  • Export daily or hourly sales.
  • Import into Excel or Google Sheets.
  • Use this as the backbone for your predictive models.

Web Analytics for Online Stores

Google Analytics (and similar tools)

If your business is online, analytics tools are already tracking visitors and sales for you.

  • Identify which pages or products get the most traffic.
  • See which days or campaigns drive more orders.
  • Use these patterns to prepare inventory and marketing in advance.

When to Consider Advanced Tools

As your data grows, you may eventually explore more advanced options such as business intelligence dashboards or machine learning platforms. However, for many small businesses, the biggest gains come simply from:

  • Saving your data consistently.
  • Visualizing patterns with charts.
  • Applying basic forecasting rules in a spreadsheet.

This website focuses on that beginner‑friendly stage — the point where predictive analytics feels achievable, not overwhelming.
