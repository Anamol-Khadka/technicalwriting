Short Case Studies

Stories of small and local businesses using data to grow smarter.

Quick visual: as predictions improve, waste shrinks and useful insight grows over time.

Case Study 1: Campus Coffee Shop

Situation: A campus coffee shop was throwing away large amounts of brewed coffee at closing time.

Action:

  • Tracked cups sold per hour for three weeks.
  • Labeled each row with day of week and any special events.

Prediction: The data showed demand dropping sharply after 6 p.m. on most weekdays.

Result:

  • They brewed smaller batches after 6 p.m.
  • Coffee waste decreased while service quality stayed the same.

Takeaway (cause–effect): Simple tracking plus pattern recognition led directly to a better brewing schedule and less waste.

Visual idea: A bar chart with coffee waste per hour before vs. after changing the brewing schedule.

Case Study 2: Neighborhood Grocery Store

Situation: A small grocery store often ran out of milk on weekends but had too much left over on weekdays.

Action:

  • Collected daily milk sales for two months.
  • Separated results into weekday vs. weekend categories.

Prediction: Weekends consistently had about 30% higher milk sales than weekdays.

Result:

  • They ordered 30% more milk for Friday–Sunday.
  • They held weekday orders steady at a lower level.

Takeaway: A simple percentage difference helped them match stock levels to real demand.

Case Study 3: Local Clothing Boutique

Situation: A clothing boutique wanted to avoid over‑ordering seasonal jackets that would later need deep discounts.

Action:

  • Reviewed last year’s jacket and sweater sales by size and style.
  • Marked the items that sold out quickly vs. those that lingered on the racks.

Prediction: Neutral colors and medium sizes sold fastest; extreme sizes and unusual colors moved slowly.

Result:

  • They ordered more of the proven bestsellers and fewer risky items.
  • End‑of‑season markdowns shrank, and profits improved.

Analogy: It’s like learning which dishes your guests always finish and cooking more of those next time.
